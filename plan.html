Plan Your Visit

  • Transportation & Airport Info: Most visitors arrive by air via a small airport accommodating jets and propeller planes. Taxis and public buses are available. Bike rentals are common and helmets are required by law.
  • Currency and Payments: Taniti uses the U.S. dollar, but euros and yen are accepted. Major credit cards are widely used and banks offer currency exchange services.
  • Local Laws and Safety: Alcohol sales are banned from midnight to 9 AM. The legal drinking age is 18, but enforcement is lax. Pickpocketing has increased with tourismâ€”remain cautious in crowds.
  • Transit and Holidays: Public buses in Taniti City run daily from 5 AM to 11 PM. Many businesses close during national holidays, so plan ahead.
  • Language and Healthcare: Many young residents speak English. Rural areas speak primarily local dialects. One hospital and several clinics serve tourists with multilingual staff.
